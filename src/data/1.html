Common Reasons People Seek Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy at NUMA:

  • General Health and Wellness: Healthy individuals seeking the benefits of physiological and therapeutic oxygen therapy
  • Sports Performance & Recovery: Enhancing athletic performance and aiding injury recovery
  • Post-Surgery Healing: Supporting recovery after surgery and improving wound healing
  • Traumatic Brain Injury & Concussions: Assisting in recovery from concussions and brain trauma
  • Sensorineural Hearing Loss: Helping individuals with hearing loss due to nerve damage
  • Inflammatory Bowel Disease: Conditions like Crohn’s disease and ulcerative colitis
  • Chronic Fatigue & Fibromyalgia: Managing symptoms of fibromyalgia, ME, and chronic fatigue syndrome
  • Stroke Rehabilitation: Promoting recovery and rehabilitation after a stroke
  • Lyme Disease: Supporting treatment for Lyme disease and related symptoms
  • Interstitial Cystitis: Aiding individuals with this painful bladder condition
  • Chronic Inflammatory Conditions: For those dealing with persistent inflammation
  • Delayed Radiation Injuries: Addressing soft tissue and bone necrosis following radiation therapy

Global Indications for Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy:

In April 2016, the European Committee for Hyperbaric Medicine (ECHM) revised their list of accepted indications for HBOT, with the final report published in March 2017. Additionally, the 13th edition of the Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy Indications from the Undersea and Hyperbaric Medical Society (UHMS) defines the globally recognized indications for HBOT, including:

  • Air or Gas Embolism
  • Carbon Monoxide Poisoning
  • Gas Gangrene
  • Compartment Syndrome
  • Crush Injury
  • Traumatic Ischemias
  • Decompression Sickness (The Bends)
  • Arterial Insufficiency
  • Severe Anemia
  • Intracranial Abscess
  • Necrotizing Soft Tissue Infection
  • Refractory Osteomyelitis
  • Delayed Radiation Injury (Soft Tissue and Bone Necrosis)
  • Compromised Grafts and Flaps
  • Acute Thermal Burns
  • Sudden Idiopathic Sensorineural Hearing Loss

NHS Coverage in the UK:

In the UK, the NHS currently funds hyperbaric oxygen therapy (HBOT) for the following conditions:

  • Decompression Illness
  • Arterial Gas Embolism
